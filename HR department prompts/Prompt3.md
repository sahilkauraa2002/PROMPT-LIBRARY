# PROMPT 3: Candidate Shortlisting
**Prompt text**

We are in the HR department of an online skincare brand and are hiring a Social Media Head. We have shortlisted a few candidates. Review the provided candidate profiles and generate a clear, professional, and structured evaluation of each candidate’s suitability for the role. The response should include a concise summary of each candidate, key strengths, and an assessment of their overall fit based on experience in social media strategy, content creation, audience engagement, and digital marketing.
Additionally, evaluate their qualifications, eligibility, and any certifications related to social media management or digital marketing, and clearly indicate how these support their suitability for the role. Ensure the evaluation is strictly based on the information provided in the profiles, avoids assumptions, and remains specific and evidence-based. Maintain a professional tone and limit the response to approximately 180–220 words while keeping the content well-organised and easy to interpret.

**Intended Workflow**

This prompt supports the candidate shortlisting stage, where HR evaluates selected candidates in more detail before interviews. It helps summarise candidate profiles and justify their selection based on role alignment. It fits between resume screening and interview scheduling, ensuring only relevant candidates move forward.

**Problem Being Solved**

Shortlisting often lacks structured comparison, leading to subjective decision-making. HR teams may struggle to clearly justify why certain candidates are selected, especially when multiple profiles appear similar. This can delay the hiring process and introduce bias. The prompt solves this by generating structured summaries, making comparisons clearer and more consistent.

**Automation Potential**

The automation potential is medium. AI can assist in organising and summarising candidate information, improving clarity and efficiency. However, human validation is required to ensure fairness and accuracy. The prompt enhances decision-making speed but does not replace HR judgment.

**Risks and Limitations**

The prompt may lead to oversimplified summaries, failing to capture deeper qualitative differences between candidates. There is also a risk of confirmation bias, where AI reinforces patterns rather than critically evaluating candidates.
Mitigation: Combine AI-generated summaries with human judgment and detailed review of candidate profiles.

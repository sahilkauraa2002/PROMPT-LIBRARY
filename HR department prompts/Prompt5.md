# PROMPT 5: Interview Question Generation
**Prompt text**

We are in the HR department of an online skincare brand and are hiring a Social Media Head. Generate 15–20 well-structured interview questions for shortlisted candidates, organised into categories such as experience, skills, qualifications, and certifications related to social media management and digital marketing. The questions should be specifically relevant to managing social media for a skincare or beauty brand and must assess practical experience, technical expertise, industry knowledge, and any relevant certifications. Include scenario-based and real-life technical questions to evaluate the candidate’s problem-solving ability in areas such as content strategy, campaign execution, audience engagement, and performance analysis. Also include a few questions around 3-5 related to the candidate’s future plans and career direction. Ensure the questions are clear, concise, non-repetitive, and directly aligned with the responsibilities of the role. Maintain a professional tone, avoid generic questions, and ensure the final output is well organised and ready to be used in an actual interview setting.

---

**Intended Workflow**

This prompt supports the interview preparation stage, where HR creates structured questions to assess candidates effectively. It ensures interviews are aligned with job requirements.

**Problem Being Solved**

Interviews often lack consistency, with questions varying across candidates. This can result in unfair evaluation and poor candidate assessment. The prompt ensures structured and relevant questions are generated.

**Automation Potential**

Automation potential is medium, as AI can generate relevant and structured questions. However, HR must review and customise them based on specific needs. It improves consistency but does not replace interviewer judgment.

**Risks and Limitations**

The AI may generate generic or repetitive questions that do not fully assess the candidate’s capabilities. There is also a risk of irrelevant or inappropriate questions if the prompt is not specific.
Mitigation: HR should review and customise questions based on role requirements and company context.

**Output**

Certainly! Below is a well-organized set of 18 interview questions for the role of Social Media Head in your skincare brand, categorized by relevant themes and tailored specifically for the industry and role.

---

### 1. **Experience**

1. Can you describe your previous experience managing social media for a skincare or beauty brand? What were the key challenges and how did you overcome them?  
2. Share an example of a successful social media campaign you led for a beauty or personal care product. What were the objectives, execution steps, and outcomes?  
3. How have you handled negative comments or a social media crisis related to product quality or customer dissatisfaction in this sector?  
4. What is your experience working with influencers or brand ambassadors in the skincare/beauty industry? How do you identify and collaborate with the right partners?  

### 2. **Skills**

5. How do you approach creating a content calendar for a skincare brand that balances educational, promotional, and engagement-driven posts?  
6. Which social media platforms have you found most effective for skincare brands, and how do you tailor content for each?  
7. Describe your process for analyzing social media metrics. What KPIs do you prioritize to measure the success of skincare campaigns?  
8. Walk me through a time when you used paid social media advertising to boost a skincare product launch. How did you optimize the campaign for best ROI?  
9. How do you ensure compliance with advertising standards and regulations related to skincare products on social media?  

### 3. **Qualifications & Certifications**

10. Do you hold any certifications relevant to digital marketing or social media management (e.g., Facebook Blueprint, Google Ads, HubSpot, Hootsuite)? How have they influenced your work?  
11. How do you stay updated on the latest social media trends and skincare industry developments? Can you give an example of how you applied a recent trend or insight in your work?  

### 4. **Scenario-Based / Problem Solving**

12. Imagine our latest serum has received mixed reviews online, with some users claiming it caused skin irritation. How would you manage the social media response and protect the brand reputation?  
13. A competitor skincare brand has launched a viral social media challenge that’s gaining massive traction. How would you respond strategically to regain audience attention?  
14. Suppose you notice a drop in engagement rates across all social platforms over two consecutive months. What specific steps would you take to diagnose and address this decline?  
15. You have a limited budget but need to increase awareness and sales for a new moisturizer targeting millennials. Outline your social media strategy and key tactics.  

### 5. **Future Plans & Career Direction**

16. Where do you see the future of social media marketing in the skincare industry heading in the next 3–5 years? How do you plan to adapt?  
17. What motivates you most about leading social media for a skincare brand specifically, and how does this role align with your long-term career goals?  
18. If hired, what would be your immediate priorities in the first 90 days to enhance our brand’s social media presence?

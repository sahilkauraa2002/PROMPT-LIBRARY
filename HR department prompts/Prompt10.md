# PROMPT 10: First Week Checklist
**Prompt text**

We are in the HR department of an online skincare brand and are onboarding a Social Media Head. Create a detailed checklist for the candidate’s first week including daily key tasks, training activities and an overview of their core responsibilities and duties of a Social Media Head. Also add something related to ice breaking activities with other staff to make a good workspace bond. Limit the checklist to a maximum of five days. Organize the checklist clearly by day, using bullet points or numbered lists. Keep the content realistic and relevant to a social media leadership role in the skincare industry. And make it on Day basis such as monday to friday.

---

**Intended Workflow**

This prompt supports onboarding planning, outlining tasks for the first week.

**Problem Being Solved**

Unstructured onboarding leads to confusion and inefficiency. This prompt ensures clarity.

**Automation Potential**

Automation potential is medium to high.

**Risks and Limitations**

The checklist may be too general and not fully aligned with real-time business needs. It may also miss organisation-specific processes.
Mitigation: Customise the checklist based on team requirements and update regularly.

**Output**

**Social Media Head - First Week Onboarding Checklist**

---

### **Day 1: Monday – Introduction & Orientation**

**Key Tasks:**
- Welcome and office tour (if applicable) or virtual introductions for remote setup.
- Set up workstations, access to all necessary tools (social media management platforms, analytics tools, email, intranet).
- Complete HR onboarding documents and review company policies.

**Training Activities:**
- Overview of company vision, mission, and skincare brand values.
- Introduction to existing social media channels and current campaign strategies.
- Review brand voice, tone, and style guidelines.
- Familiarize with target audiences and customer personas.

**Ice-breaking Activity:**
- Casual meet-and-greet lunch or virtual coffee with the marketing and creative teams.
- Quick “Two Truths and a Lie” game to learn fun facts about team members.

---

### **Day 2: Tuesday – Understanding Current Social Media Landscape**

**Key Tasks:**
- Deep dive into current social media performance reports (engagement, reach, conversions).
- Review competitor social media presence and benchmarking.
- Analyze recent campaigns: What worked? What didn’t?

**Training Activities:**
- Training on social media analytics platforms used by the company (e.g., Sprout Social, Hootsuite, Google Analytics).
- Overview of content calendar management and scheduling tools.

**Ice-breaking Activity:**
- Join the daily team stand-up meeting; share a brief personal introduction and social media background.
- Participate in a quick brainstorming session about potential content ideas based on competitor insights.

---

### **Day 3: Wednesday – Strategy & Planning**

**Key Tasks:**
- Collaborate with marketing, product, and creative teams to understand upcoming product launches or promotions.
- Begin drafting a 90-day social media strategy aligned with business goals.
- Identify key performance indicators (KPIs) for social media success.

**Training Activities:**
- Workshop on crisis management and social media policy compliance.
- Review customer feedback and testimonials from social channels.

**Ice-breaking Activity:**
- Organize a “Show & Tell” where team members share their favorite skincare products and why.
- Informal group walk or stretch break to build rapport.

---

### **Day 4: Thursday – Content & Campaign Development**

**Key Tasks:**
- Review and approve content drafts prepared by the creative team.
- Plan influencer and partnership outreach strategies.
- Set up initial campaign briefs for upcoming promotions.

**Training Activities:**
- Session on paid social media advertising best practices and budget management.
- Training on legal and ethical guidelines specific to the skincare industry (e.g., ingredient claims, testimonials).

**Ice-breaking Activity:**
- Host a quick “Ask Me Anything” (AMA) session with the Social Media Head, encouraging open dialogue.
- Share favorite skincare tips or routines to foster team connection.

---

### **Day 5: Friday – Review & Team Integration**

**Key Tasks:**
- Present initial findings and draft strategy to the marketing leadership team for feedback.
- Establish regular reporting cadence and communication channels.
- Set goals for the next two weeks and outline priorities.

**Training Activities:**
- Overview of collaboration tools used across departments (Slack, Trello, Asana).
- Training on customer service standards for social media interactions.

**Ice-breaking Activity:**
- Organize a casual end-of-week virtual or in-person happy hour or team breakfast.
- Group discussion on company culture and ways to enhance collaboration.

---

### **Core Responsibilities & Duties Overview**

- **Strategic Leadership:** Develop and execute comprehensive social media strategies aligned with brand goals.
- **Content Oversight:** Guide content creation ensuring brand consistency and engagement.
- **Team Management:** Lead social media team, fostering creativity, accountability, and professional growth.
- **Analytics & Reporting:** Monitor, analyze, and report on social media metrics to optimize campaigns.
- **Community Engagement:** Manage customer interactions, respond to inquiries, and build brand loyalty.
- **Collaboration:** Work cross-functionally with marketing, product, creative, and customer service teams.
- **Compliance:** Ensure all social media activities adhere to industry regulations and company policies.
- **Innovation:** Stay updated with social media trends and emerging platforms to maintain competitive advantage.

---

This checklist will help the new Social Media Head acclimate smoothly, understand their role deeply, and build strong relationships within the team right from the start.
